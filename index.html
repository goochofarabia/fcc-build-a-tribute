Here's a timeline of Einstein's life:

  • March 14, 1879 - Born in Ulm, Kingdom of Württemberg, German Empire
  • 1880 - Family moved to Munich, where his father and uncle ran an electrical engineering business
  • 1894 - Left Germany to avoid mandatory military service; moved to Switzerland
  • 1896 - Renounced German citizenship and enrolled at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich
  • 1900 - Graduated as a physics and mathematics teacher
  • 1902 - Began work as a patent clerk at the Swiss Patent Office in Bern
  • 1905 - Annus Mirabilis (“Miracle Year”): Published four groundbreaking papers, including:
    • Special theory of relativity
    • "Explanation of the photoelectric effect
    • Mass-energy equivalence (E = mc²)
  • 1909 - Appointed associate professor at the University of Zurich
  • 1914 - Moved to Berlin to work at the Prussian Academy of Sciences
  • 1915 - Completed the General Theory of Relativity
  • 1919 - Theory of relativity confirmed during a solar eclipse; became internationally famous
  • 1921 - Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics (for the photoelectric effect, not relativity)
  • 1933 - Emigrated to the United States after Adolf Hitler rose to power
  • 1933 - Accepted a position at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey
  • 1939 - Signed a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt warning of the potential development of atomic weapons
  • 1940 - Became a U.S. citizen (while retaining Swiss citizenship)
  • 1952 - Offered the presidency of Israel; declined the position
  • April 18, 1955 - Died in Princeton, New Jersey, at age 76
